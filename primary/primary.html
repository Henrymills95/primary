Impact of the Primary School Curriculum on Our Learners

At MPIS, the Primary School curriculum is designed to build upon the strong foundation laid at the preschool level.

Guided by Ghana‚Äôs Standards-Based Curriculum, it promotes critical thinking, creativity, and strong academic competence .Through structured lessons, group work, and practical projects, students develop literacy, numeracy, and problem-solving skills that are essential for everyday life and future academic success.

Performance at the Primary School Level

Our Primary School students consistently demonstrate academic excellence and progressive skill development across all subjects. Learners are encouraged to be active participants in their education through continuous assessments, projects, and interactive teaching methodologies.

The consistent success of our pupils in internal evaluations and inter-school academic competitions reflects the strength of our teaching and learning process. This level serves as a critical growth phase, where learners mature academically and socially in preparation for Junior High School.

Dedicated and Professional Staff

The quality of education at our Primary School is deeply rooted in the professionalism and passion of our teaching staff. Our educators are trained to understand and respond to the developmental needs of children in this age group.

They employ diverse instructional strategies to cater to different learning styles and abilities, ensuring no child is left behind. Regular training, mentorship, and collaborative planning empower our staff to deliver the curriculum with clarity, creativity, and care, resulting in meaningful learning experiences.

Preparing Learners for the Next Level

The Primary School phase at MPIS is strategically structured to prepare learners for the rigors of Junior High School. With a balanced focus on academics, life skills, and moral development, our pupils transition with readiness and resilience.
